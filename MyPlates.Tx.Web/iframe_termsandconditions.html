Although any Texas resident may place an order for Plates through the WSF, you or the person for whom you order them (for example, as a gift) will not be able to use them on a vehicle unless you (or that person) meet the eligibility requirements prescribed by the Texas Department of Transportation ("TxDOT"). When you use the WSF to order Plates for use on a vehicle, you are confirming that:

  • The person on whose vehicle the Plates will be used is a Texas resident, and the title to the vehicle is a Texas title.
  • The Texas title to the vehicle on which the Plates will be used is either:
  1. in your name (may be a joint title with another individual), OR
  2. in the name of the person for whom you order them (may be a joint title with another individual), OR
  3. in the name of a third party (for example, the lessor of the vehicle), AND the person who will use the Plates (a) has the legal right to posses and control the vehicle (b) uses the vehicle for personal use, and (c) will execute an affidavit to that effect and present the affidavit to the county tax office when retrieving the Plates.   3. when you collect your Plates from the county tax assessor-collector, the county tax assessor collector may charge an additional, pro-rated vehicle registration fee to synchronize your vehicle registration period with the first annual period for which you have obtained the Plates.
